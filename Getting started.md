# Getting started

*Create an app or game that feels at home on every platform you support.*

- [Design principles](Getting%20started/Design%20principles.md) — Explore fundamental principles that guide design across Apple platforms.
- [Designing for iOS](Getting%20started/Designing%20for%20iOS.md) — People depend on their iPhone to help them stay connected, play games, view media, accomplish tasks, and track personal data in any location and while on the go.
- [Designing for iPadOS](Getting%20started/Designing%20for%20iPadOS.md) — People value the power, mobility, and flexibility of iPad as they enjoy media, play games, perform detailed productivity tasks, and bring their creations to life.
- [Designing for macOS](Getting%20started/Designing%20for%20macOS.md) — People rely on the power, spaciousness, and flexibility of a Mac as they perform in-depth productivity tasks, view media or content, and play games, often using several apps at once.
- [Designing for tvOS](Getting%20started/Designing%20for%20tvOS.md) — People enjoy the vibrant content, immersive experiences, and streamlined interactions that tvOS delivers in media and games, as well as in fitness, education, and home utility apps.
- [Designing for visionOS](Getting%20started/Designing%20for%20visionOS.md) — When people wear Apple Vision Pro, they enter an infinite 3D space where they can engage with your app or game while staying connected to their surroundings.
- [Designing for watchOS](Getting%20started/Designing%20for%20watchOS.md) — When people glance at their Apple Watch, they know they can access essential information and perform simple, timely tasks whether they’re stationary or in motion.
- [Designing for games](Getting%20started/Designing%20for%20games.md) — When people play your game on an Apple device, they dive into the world you designed while relying on the platform features they love.
